PCube Foundation Website Strategy & Content
Plan
Executive Summary: PCube Foundation (Prospect • Progress • Play) is a Section 8 sports NGO bridging
India’s grassroots talent to national opportunities. Our website will inspire youth athletes (12–20),
rural communities, and sponsors/CSR donors by showcasing PCube’s mission, impact, and pathways.
This report outlines a complete website content strategy: site architecture, full-page draft copy, brand
elements (taglines, pillars, tone), SEO plans, CTAs/microcopy, interactive feature content, a 6‑month
content calendar, social templates, imagery ideas, a feature-comparison table, and an implementation
roadmap with effort estimates. The approach follows NGO best practices (clear mission, impact
storytelling, calls-to-action) and sports development examples .
1. Site Architecture & Page Purposes
We recommend a clear, user-friendly structure. Each main page serves a distinct audience need and
action:
Home: Hero message + mission; snapshot of impact (statistics); quick CTAs (Apply, Sponsor,
Donate); featured athlete story or video.
About: PCube’s story, mission, values, leadership (founder/coach intro), timeline. Builds
credibility (new visitors first stop) .
Programs: Overview of sports programs (e.g. Hockey Training Academy, Grassroots Camps,
Equipment Distribution). Each with description and outcomes. (Showcase how we train & develop
talent, per our “Progress” pillar).
Athlete Profiles: Stories of current students. Each profile includes background, training
journey, achievements (e.g. district/state selections). Builds emotional connection and proof of
success .
Impact: Key metrics (e.g. athletes trained, matches played, tournaments reached, equipment
distributed) in infographics; case studies of champions. Demonstrates transparency and success
to donors .
Sponsor/Partner: Why sponsor us? Benefits (CSR alignment, brand exposure, social impact).
Logos of supporters; “Partner with Us” details. (Use inspiring appeals – e.g. “Become the
champion behind their dreams” as used by similar NGOs .)
Donate / Sponsor-a-Player: Options to donate or sponsor specific items/players. Suggested
donation tiers (e.g. ₹1000 equips one athlete). Clear CTAs (“Sponsor a Player”, “Donate Now”).
Talent Application: Online form for aspiring athletes (upload video/details). Encourages youth
engagement. (“Think you have what it takes?” prompt).
Blog/Stories: News, training tips, event recaps, press releases. Keeps content fresh and SEOfriendly.
Contact: Contact info (address, email, phone), social links, simple contact form.
Footer: Site map (Home, About, Programs, Impact, Sponsors, Donate, Apply, Blog, Contact),
social icons, newsletter signup, legal (Privacy/Terms).
Each page will follow nonprofit best practices: concise mission on homepage, clear navigation,
accessible design , and prominent CTAs (donate, apply) easily found within 7 seconds .

For Athletes: “Get world-class training, gear, and a clear path to compete – we turn your passion

3 Brand Pillars (Prospect, Progress, Play):
Prospect: Identifying raw talent early (scouting camps).
Progress: Structured coaching and mentorship (skill building at every stage).
Play: Creating opportunities to compete and win (tournaments, matches).
These pillars should be repeated in copy (e.g. “PCube’s Prospect program finds talent…”,
“Through Progress our trainees improve skills,” “We love to Play by competing nationally.”).
Tone of Voice: Energetic, hopeful, and credible. Speak directly and vividly to youth and
sponsors – use active, motivational language (e.g. “you can achieve”, “join us”, “together we will”).
Maintain professionalism (not overly casual) to convey seriousness. Let PCube’s passion shine
through (per nonprofit content advice: “Let your nonprofit’s personality and voice shine” ).
Use inclusive language (“we” and “you”), keep it clear and concise with an inspiring edge.
Key Messaging:
For Athletes: “PCube is your partner – we believe in your talent and give you the tools to succeed.
No fees. No barriers. Only opportunity.” Use emotion and aspiration. (“Imagine playing at
national championships – PCube helps get you there.”)
For Sponsors/CSR: “Align with a proven program that transforms lives through sports . Your
support builds leadership skills, national pride, and fulfils CSR goals. We ensure transparency
and recognition for your impact.” Highlight shared values, ROI of social change, and gratitude.

CTAs (consistent, action-focused): “Apply as Athlete”, “Sponsor an Athlete”, “Donate Now”, “Partner
with Us”, “Learn More”, “Get Involved”. Use buttons with strong contrast (per accessibility) .

On forms: “Your Full Name”, “Enter your email”, “Select your program”, “Choose sponsorship amount”,

6. Interactive Features Content & Flows
6.1 Athlete Journey Tracker
Purpose: Show each athlete’s progression visually (from local fields to national). Engages visitors by
making success tangible.
UX Copy:
Title: “Your Journey So Far” (if personalized) or “PCube Athlete Journey”.
Step labels: “Local Tryouts → PCube Training → State Team → National Trials”.
Each step has a short note, e.g.: “Local School Trials: Scouted from a village school in Nashik.”
Mouse-over/pop-up details for each stage: date, key event (e.g. “June 2025: Selected for district
team”).
Sample Data Flow:
Visitor clicks an athlete (e.g. from Athlete Profile) and sees a timeline:
Village Level: June 2023 – Participated in community hockey camp.
PCube Academy: Sept 2023 – Enrolled in PCube training, weekly practice.
District Team: Mar 2024 – Made the district under-16 team.
State Trials: Dec 2024 – Invited to state trials.

6.2 Sponsor-a-Player System
Content: A searchable list of athletes (photos, name, age, sport). For each: summary (“Needs
equipment for State Championship – ₹3,000”).
UX steps:
1. Browse Athletes: Visitors see thumbnail cards: e.g. “Ravi (14, Pune) – Needs hockey stick & shoes.”
2. Select Player: Click to view profile/details & sponsorship options.
3. Choose Support: Options: Equipment Sponsorship (₹X), Tournament Fee (₹Y), or custom donation.
4. Checkout: Friendly donation form; messaging like “You are becoming the champion behind
[Athlete’s] dreams.”
5. Confirmation & Impact: Thank-you page showing summary of what was sponsored (e.g. “You
covered a full kit for Ravi!”).
UX Copy Examples:
On listing: “Sponsor gear for Sarita – outfielder dreams of state tryouts.”
On donation form: “Enter amount to empower this athlete:”, “Add a note (optional): ‘Keep shining,
[Athlete Name]!”.
On confirmation: “Thank you! You just equipped an athlete for success.”
6.3 Athlete Potential Analyzer (AI Tool)
Purpose: Fun interactive quiz that suggests a player’s potential and recommended program. Great
engagement “wow” feature.
UX Flow:
1. Start Screen: “Calculate Your Sports Potential”.
2. Input Form: Fields like Age, Height, Sport Interest (Hockey), Current Level (Beginner/Intermediate),
Fitness Level (1–5).
3. Analyze: Button “See My Potential”.
4. Result Page: “Your Potential Score: 78/100 – High!” or “Moderate” with advice.
If high: “You have natural talent! PCube recommends our advanced academy track. Apply now to
fast-track your journey.”
If moderate: “With regular training you can excel. Try our grassroots camps to improve your
skills.”
Include a progress bar or graphic.
Sample Text:
“Great news, [Name]! Your profile shows excellent agility and passion. You could reach the state level.
Join our PCube Academy for focused training.”
“You scored 58/100. A bit more training needed — our beginner hockey camps will help you improve.”
